What is I/O?

I/O is an autonomous technology project connecting grassroots organizations, nonprofits, and community members with free resources on technology, such as info sessions, educational resources, and workshops.

Each week, we build space for individuals and small organizations to become more aware of the power in their personal devices, and to practice becoming more competent in utilizing that power for the betterment and autonomization of their own lives and their community.

All of our workshops are free to attend, with a suggested donation of $10.

Here you will find links to helpful resources, as well as instructions on how to set up a calendar to stay subscribed to our events.

Our Programs

We host programs (nearly) every Sunday from 2–6pm at The Footlight at 465 Seneca Ave, Ridgewood Queens, NY 11385. We trade off between programming that is generally oriented towards Individuals (I) and programming that is generally oriented towards organizations (O) or collectives.

Of course, anyone can attend either type of gathering — this distinction is made only to help you scope what you would like to attend, and the things you would like to learn. When the topic appeals to both, the event will be marked "I/O."

Our Ethics

In computing, I/O stands for "input/output," and is used regularly across a variety of contexts, but almost always refers to some kind of exchange between external devices and internal devices, or, moreover, between machines and humans. It is this very exchange we concern ourselves with.

Io is also the name of Jupiter's innermost moon, named after a mortal who befriends imprisoned Prometheus in Greek mythology, and whose descendent, Heracles, eventually frees him.

Our programs are not part of a "bootcamp" or coding school.

We are not recruiters. We actively discourage anyone from getting into the so-called "Tech Industry."

We are anti-technocapitalist. Our ethics place us staunchly and clearly against "Adtech" and "Fintech" and Silicon Valley.

We do believe technology is intangible earth, with which we can and should have an earthly relationship. It is power that may be harnessed through holistic methods by individuals and their networks for the empowerment of oneself and of one's actual communities.

Our approach stands in contrast to sterility, stagnancy, and authoritativeness by being participatory, flexible, and adaptive.

If you work in the Tech Industry, please be advised that our ideology is directly hostile to Silicon Valley profit motives.

We have a strict no-tolerance policy against bigotry of any kind. Racism, queerphobia, transphobia, sexism, or any kind of similarly awful behavior will result in immediate removal from the venue and you will not be asked back. Starting arguments that hijack the experience of others or make other people feel less able to learn will result in the same, and this includes being condescending to others.
